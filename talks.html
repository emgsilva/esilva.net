Here you can find some of my most recent talks/presentations (for a full list check Speakerdeck and Slideshare). I am continuously working on these and new talks related with the topics I work and am passionate about (e.g., Technical Leadership and Architecture modernization, Team Topologies and Effective Enabling Teams, Sociotechnical Systems Evolution and Architecture, and many related topics around the ideas of helping organizations achieve a "Sustainable Fast Flow of Change"). I am available to give these talks (and extended/adjusted versions with Q&A) on different public of company events. Feel free to reach out for more details.

Highlighted Talks:
  • Towards Architecture Topologies for Sustainable Fast Flow of Change (Keynote) - In this talk, I motivate the need for organizations to embrace uncertainty and unpredictability, as these are unavoidable for most modern organizations building software. I share different thinking models, practices, and examples of nurturing structures and approaches to learning, design, and decision-making, which help move towards a sustainable fast flow of change. In particular, I discuss the idea of Architecture Topologies, a thinking model, and a set of practices I have been developing to help us evolve and improve how we organize and approach architecture.
  • Supporting a Sustainable Fast Flow of Change with Effective Enabling Teams - In this talk I highlight the importance of Enabling Teams to support sustainable fast flow in modern organizations. I highlight why Enabling Teams are essential to accelerate effective learning and addressing missing capabilities in organizations. Then I share the key phases I am seeing recurring in organizations that are adopting successfully adopting Enabling Teams. I do this by sharing first-hand examples and experiences.
